## New post

**Dispatch Nº 53 — "The order change that races your warehouse"**
`the-order-change-that-races-your-warehouse.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-08 · ~1,700 words

Chosen because the post-purchase order-change request ("can you change my order?") is a
high-frequency, heavily complained-about Shopify pain on the community forums and merchant
subreddits, and it wasn't covered by the existing six dispatches (address validation, fraud flag,
overselling, WISMO, the field study, the lead essay). Angle: after checkout Shopify gives the buyer
no self-serve way to fix a typo, swap a size/variant, change quantity, add an item, or update the
shipping address; the merchant's edit window closes the moment fulfilment starts (and the billing
address can never be edited), so a trivial change becomes a footrace against your own warehouse.

Statistics are sourced from current web research, not invented: ~60–80% of ecommerce tickets are
automatable (order changes among the largest); ~69% of shoppers prefer self-serve; structured
self-serve / automated editing cuts order-change tickets ~40–60%; Shopify locks most edits at
"Fulfil items" and never allows billing-address edits.
