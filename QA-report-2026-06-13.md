checks caught the site mid-publish (the post existed on disk and in the homepage JSON-LD, but was

## Fixed

- **Meta description too long on the new post.** `the-restock-alert-that-never-fires.html` had a
  167-character `<meta name="description">`, over the ~160-char SERP limit that every other page
  respects. Trimmed to 149 chars without losing meaning (dropped "the item returned" so it reads
  "…the customer who asked never hears and a competitor sells it instead. The AI playbook that
  fixes it"). No em/en dashes introduced; the og/twitter descriptions are independent and already
  fine, so they were left untouched. All ten meta descriptions are now in the 100–160 range.

## Concurrency note

`sitemap.xml`, `feed.xml`, `llms.txt`, and the older post files being rewritten within the QA
window. To avoid colliding with the live writer, no edits were made until the working tree was
stable for several consecutive samples. By the time QA settled, the concurrent run had already
added the homepage card, bumped the counts, updated the feed/sitemap/llms, and inserted the
Read-next back-links — so the only thing left for QA to fix was the over-length meta description.
