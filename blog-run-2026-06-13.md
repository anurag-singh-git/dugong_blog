## New post

**Dispatch Nº 56 — "The restock alert that never fires"**
`the-restock-alert-that-never-fires.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-13 · ~1,790 words declared

Chosen because back-in-stock / restock notification is one of the most-asked, most-assumed
Shopify questions on the community forums and merchant subreddits, and it was not yet covered by
the existing eight dispatches. The recurring forum pattern is specific: merchants assume "notify
me when available" is a native setting, discover it is not, and are told to export the email list
and message people by hand. It is distinct from the overselling post (Nº about inventory accuracy
at fulfilment): this one is about the post-stockout demand-recovery loop, the waitlist, the
priority order, the throttle, and the channel.

The Shopify-specific limitation is confirmed from current research: Shopify has no built-in feature
that emails or texts customers when an out-of-stock product is restocked; some themes show a basic
"notify me" form, but it only collects an email and does not watch inventory or send, so the
standard workaround is a manual export or a third-party app.

Statistics are sourced from current web research, not invented: stockouts account for ~40% of lost
sales; the lost sales ratio averages ~20-30% in ecommerce; ~69% of online shoppers buy from a
competitor rather than wait for a restock; a stocked-out product stays unavailable for an average
of ~35 days. These are presented as ranges/approximations in the body.

The angle the piece adds beyond "use an app": the real job is judgement in the middle, notify in
fair order (first-requested-first), meter to the units that actually landed, pick SMS vs email per
subscriber, deep-link to the variant, throttle the next wave, stop on sellthrough, and escalate
hyped drops or tiny restocks to a human. Two failure modes are called out that a naive blast
creates: selling the restock to whoever clicks fastest, and overselling when the alert fires off an
unsynced count.
