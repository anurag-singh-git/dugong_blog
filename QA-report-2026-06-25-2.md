## Issue found and fixed

**Em dashes in homepage authored prose.** The two lead essays and the excerpt cards on
`index.html` carried 15 em dashes inside running sentences, which conflicts with the standing
no-em-dash style the daily post QA enforces on new content. Replaced each with grammatically
appropriate punctuation (comma, colon, or semicolon), preserving every word:

- Lead-essay dek: "the problem — and" → comma.
- Excerpt cards: "two weeks later — return fee" → colon; "evidence hidden — a coin-flip" → comma;
  "dread most — because" → comma; "ecommerce — ~18%" → colon; "Returns and refunds — ... — are
  under 1%" → paired commas; "automation — and how clearer" → comma; "sheet tabs — they're" →
  colon.
- Nº47 lead essay: "year over year — but" → comma; "same model — a different" → comma; "and —
  critically — an" → paired commas; "the easy part — what's hard" → semicolon; "right
  representation — complex state machines" → colon; "workflows — in any tool — and" → paired
  commas.

Post-fix verification: `index.html` div balance still 173/173, JSON-LD still parses, and the only
em dashes remaining are template-level and cross-page-consistent (the `<title>`/`og`/`twitter`
title suffix, the RSS alternate-link title, three CSS comments, the two ticker `VOL. 04 — 2026`
lines, the two `DISPATCH Nº 47 — MAY 28, 2026` byline lines, and the styled `— A. Rao` card
attribution). These match the shared template used by all 21 posts and were intentionally left so
the homepage stays consistent with every post file.
