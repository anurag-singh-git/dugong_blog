## New post

**Dispatch Nº 73 — "The card-testing attack that floods your store overnight"**
`the-card-testing-attack-that-floods-your-store.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-07-01 · ~2,260 words

Chosen because card testing (carding) is one of the loudest, most-repeated complaints in Shopify
merchant communities and none of the existing 26 dispatches covered it. It validates strongly on
public forums: the Shopify Community has live threads titled "Bunches of orders coming in with small
amounts, all frauds" and "Card testing bot attack flooding our store with hundreds of fake abandoned
checkouts, no way to remove them." The recurring story is identical every time: hundreds of tiny
orders overnight, different IPs, names, and cards, one or two cheap products, most declined and a few
approved, with no obvious button to make it stop. Merchants report raising the item price and the bot
buying it anyway, and getting no resolution from support, fraud apps, card companies, or the
authorities.

The native gap is real and specific. Shopify Payments runs machine-learning models that can trigger a
checkout CAPTCHA, which stops some crude bots but is bypassed when the attacker rotates IPs and spaces
out attempts, so waves still get through. The cleanup (cancel, refund before ship, restock) is manual,
one order at a time against a fraud clock. The rule tooling to auto-cancel or auto-flag lives in
Shopify Flow, which is only on the Advanced and Plus plans, and even there Flow reacts one order at a
time and cannot read a burst as a burst. The post frames the differentiator: not "block bots" but
"notice a burst is an attack, cancel and refund the junk, keep it out of your metrics, slow the
source without punishing real buyers, and alert a human once with the mess handled."

The heading screams the use case for anyone living it ("the card-testing attack that floods your store
overnight") and carries high-intent phrasing: "Shopify card testing," "card testing attack Shopify,"
"Shopify fake orders," "Shopify bot orders," "carding," "block bot orders Shopify," "cancel fraud
orders Shopify," and "Shopify abandoned checkout bot."

It is deliberately adjacent to but distinct from the two existing fraud-adjacent posts: the high-risk
flag post (interpreting Shopify's single-order risk score) and the chargeback post (fighting a dispute
after the fact). This post is about the coordinated overnight flood itself, which it links as its
closest cousins along with the unpaid-order and order-change posts for the cleanup-under-time-pressure
angle.
