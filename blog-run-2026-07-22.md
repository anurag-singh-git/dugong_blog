feature request "Built-in duplicate customer account detection and bulk merge tool." Third-party

content. No existing dispatch covered it: the closest posts (segment repeat customers, combine
multiple orders, limit purchase quantity per customer) all depend on customer identity but none
addresses the identity fragmentation itself.

The native gap is real, specific, and credited: Shopify enforces one profile per email, surfaces
similar profiles, and has a real two-profile merge (More actions > Merge customer, or select two
on the Customers page). The walls: (1) exactly two at a time, no bulk select, no store-wide
duplicate report, no queue; (2) shallow detection that cannot see email variants, relay
addresses, or email-less POS profiles sharing a card and postcode with an online account;
(3) merge refused when either profile has a subscription contract (current or past), a B2B
company link, a vaulted payment card, a store credit account, or a multipass login (facts
verified against Shopify help docs and Recharge/third-party documentation); (4) Shopify Flow
has no merge customers action. Why it matters: split LTV so VIP segments miss members, welcome
discounts re-issued to regulars, doubled campaign sends, support blind to history, inflated
new-customer counts and understated repeat rate. The judgement framing: same name is not same
person, wrong merges are privacy incidents with no unmerge, and consent must never be upgraded
by a merge.

The pain-point heading screams the use case ("the customer Shopify counts three times"); the H1
carries the high-intent search phrasing ("How to merge duplicate customer profiles on Shopify in
bulk"). Target phrases: "Shopify merge duplicate customers in bulk," "Shopify duplicate customer
profiles," "Shopify merge customer accounts," "Shopify bulk merge customers," "Shopify customer
merge not working," "Shopify POS duplicate customers," "Shopify guest checkout duplicate
accounts," "clean up Shopify customer list."
