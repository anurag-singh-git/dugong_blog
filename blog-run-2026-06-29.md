## New post

**Dispatch Nº 71 — "The payout that never matches your sales"**
`the-payout-that-never-matches-your-sales.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-29 · ~2,572 words

Chosen because Shopify payout reconciliation is the single most-complained-about ecommerce
bookkeeping pain and none of the existing 24 dispatches touched it. It validates strongly on public
forums: accounting and QuickBooks Online communities (and Reddit's r/quickbooksonline) repeatedly
flag Shopify payout misrecording as the most common ecommerce bookkeeping error, the "click Add and
double your revenue" trap is the canonical mistake, and a whole app category (A2X, Webgility, Link My
Books, reconcile.ly, SaasAnt) exists purely to summarize a payout and make it match the bank deposit.
The recurring story is a merchant staring at a bank deposit that is smaller than their sales and not
knowing how to record it without either double counting revenue or losing the fees.

The native gap is confirmed and concrete: a Shopify payout is gross sales netted down by processing
fees, refunds, chargebacks and their fees, adjustments, and a held reserve, and it is paid in rolling
batches that cross calendar days, so one deposit can represent dozens or hundreds of orders and never
equals a day's sales. The Payouts page reports the split and exports a CSV but posts nothing to the
books and matches nothing to the bank feed; the official QuickBooks and Xero connectors sync
order-level data but do not split a batched, netted payout or tie it to the deposit, so they either
record the deposit as fresh income (double counting revenue already booked) or leave order income and
an unmatched deposit that never reconcile; and Shopify Flow has no action that decomposes a payout,
matches a deposit, or writes a journal entry. Multiple gateways (PayPal, Shop Pay Installments) each
deposit and net fees on their own schedule, multiplying the problem.

The heading screams the use case for anyone living it ("the payout that never matches your sales"),
and carries the high-intent searched phrasing around "Shopify payout doesn't match sales," "reconcile
Shopify payments QuickBooks," "reconcile Shopify Xero," "Shopify deposit smaller than sales," and
"Shopify payout reconciliation."

It is deliberately adjacent to but distinct from the money-flow posts already live: the chargeback
post (money leaving on a deadline) and the return post (refunds), which this post links as its closest
cousins. The differentiator beyond "install a reconciliation app": not booking every deposit the same
way, but decomposing each payout into sales/fees/refunds/chargebacks/tax, matching it to the orders
and to the bank deposit to the cent, splitting each gateway, and holding any deposit that does not tie
out for a human instead of forcing it to balance.
