## New post

**Dispatch Nº 77 — "The product drop that won't go live on time"**
`how-to-schedule-product-publishing-on-shopify.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-07-05 · ~2,158 words

Chosen because "schedule product publishing / auto-unpublish products on a date" is one of the
most-repeated, still-unsolved requests in Shopify merchant communities, and none of the existing
posts covered it. Forum validation is strong and specific: recurring Shopify Community and
Developer Community threads ("Does product scheduling apply to all sales channels," "Can't schedule
publish in BULK EDIT for different sales channels," "schedule availability reverting") plus a large
cluster of third-party guides (Matrixify, Mechanic, Arigato, DataEase) all describe the same wall.

The native gap is real and specific, and the post is careful to credit the part Shopify does. Its
built-in future publishing genuinely schedules a product going live, but only to the **Online
Store** channel (never POS, the Buy Button, or other channels), it **no-ops if the product is still
in Draft** at the scheduled minute, it **cannot bulk-schedule** across channels in the native
admin, and, most tellingly, it will only ever schedule a product going **live**, never coming
**down**. So ending a flash sale, a time-boxed collab, or a seasonal line falls to a human with a
calendar reminder. The post frames the differentiator: not one more date picker but a readiness
check plus a timed publish across every channel plus a timed take-down that runs both ways: read
the launch set by tag or collection, confirm each product is truly ready, publish everywhere at the
minute, unpublish when the window closes, protect the retired URL, keep the set in sync, and hold
the judgement calls (a draft at go-live, a stockless drop, a clashing launch time) for a human.

Distinct from its nearest cousins: the schedule-a-sale post is about **price/discount** timing and
reverting, not product visibility; the hide-sold-out-variants and push-sold-out-to-bottom posts are
**inventory-driven** visibility, not calendar-driven publish/unpublish across channels.

Heading screams the use case ("the product drop that won't go live on time"); the H1 carries the
high-intent search phrasing ("How to automatically schedule product publishing on Shopify").
Target phrases: "Shopify schedule product publish," "schedule product publishing Shopify," "auto
publish products Shopify," "schedule unpublish Shopify," "Shopify future publishing all channels,"
"bulk schedule products Shopify," "Shopify product drop schedule," and "schedule product launch
Shopify."
