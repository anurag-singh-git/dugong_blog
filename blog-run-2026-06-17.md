## New post

**Dispatch Nº 61 — "The meta description you write one at a time"**
`the-meta-description-you-write-one-at-a-time.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-17 · ~1,880 words declared

Chosen because the unique meta description is a real, recurring Shopify SEO complaint that no native
feature closes, and it was not yet covered by the existing 14 dispatches. It is distinct from the
adjacent commerce posts: the sold-out post (Nº 55) is merchandising of items out of stock, and the
general field study (Nº 47) is about which automations merchants build. This post is the content/SEO
chore itself: writing a distinct, intent-matched meta description for every product, collection, and
page when Shopify offers no way to do it in bulk without making them all identical.

The Shopify-specific limitations are confirmed from current research and form the crux:
(1) Shopify's bulk editor exposes the meta description field, but the only bulk action sets the SAME
text across every selected product, which is exactly the duplicate/boilerplate text search guidance
tells you to avoid, so the "shortcut" produces a worse result than doing nothing. (2) A unique
description must be written in each item's search engine listing one product at a time. (3) Leaving
the field blank does not mean nothing: Shopify auto-generates the SERP snippet from the page content
(usually the opening of the product description), which is often truncated mid-sentence and nearly
identical across similar products. (4) Every surface (products, collections, homepage, standard
pages, blog posts) has its own field, edited individually, and the high-intent collection pages are
the ones most often left blank. CSV/spreadsheet round-trips are bulk for moving the data but still
require authoring each unique line by hand, and a careless re-import can clobber neighboring fields;
template SEO apps produce lines that are technically unique (product name differs) but functionally
identical (same mould). The crowded field of SEO apps is itself evidence of the native gap. There is
even a community thread titled, almost verbatim, "can you fix meta descriptions in bulk so they don't
all sound the same."

Facts are sourced from current web research, not invented, and presented as approximations: the
~150-160 character display window (desktop truncates past it, mobile shows less, Google does not
penalize length), and the guidance that uniqueness and search-intent matching matter more than the
exact count. The GEO angle is woven in: answer engines and AI search lean on a concise, distinct
line to decide what a page is about and what to quote, so a blank or duplicated meta gives them
nothing to distinguish.

The angle beyond "install an SEO app": writing the meta was never a data problem, it is a reading
and judgement problem wearing a data problem's clothes. The real job is to find every missing,
duplicated, or wrong-length description, read each page to write a line that fits only it, hold the
length window and brand voice, guarantee no two are identical or templated, fix weak meta titles in
the same pass, write back through the API without disturbing price or inventory, and re-check new
products so the field never silently drifts back to blank. A callout warns that the blank field is
the quiet one: a generic snippet you shipped without reading.
