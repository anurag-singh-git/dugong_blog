## New post

**Dispatch Nº 83 — "The bundle sale that never touches your component stock"**
`how-to-sync-bundle-inventory-with-component-stock-on-shopify.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-07-15 · ~2,153 words

Chosen because "bundle inventory not updating," "how do I sell a bundle of two products and make
the inventory go down," "my bundle shows sold out but the items are in stock," and "components not
deducting when the bundle sells" are among the most-repeated, still-unsolved complaints for any
store selling kits, gift sets, or mix-and-match packs. Forum and vendor validation is strong: the
Shopify community threads ask for it verbatim, and a whole category of bundle/inventory-sync apps
(Simple Bundles, Sumtracker, and similar) exists precisely because the native platform does not do
component-level tracking well. No existing dispatch covered it: the closest posts
(prevent-overselling-across-channels, set-inventory-reorder-points, hide-sold-out-variants) are
channel-level sync, reorder timing, and storefront merchandising, not the bundle-to-component
inventory relationship.

The native gap is real and specific, and the post credits what Shopify does. The free Shopify
Bundles app genuinely links a fixed bundle to its components and shows availability from the
lowest-stock component, which handles the simple case. But (1) it caps components/variants and does
not track true mix-and-match inventory cleanly; (2) it tracks against overall component stock, not
per location, so a two-warehouse store can sell a bundle a location cannot assemble; (3) it does
nothing for the large number of bundles built the older way as a standalone product/variant with
its own inventory number, which no component sale ever touches; and (4) order edits and channel
sync break even a linked bundle (the bundle usually travels to a marketplace as one atomic SKU, so
components show phantom stock). Even after Shopify's Spring 2026 inventory updates, multi-SKU
component tracking remains unsolved per the vendor coverage. The post frames the differentiator:
treat every component as the source of truth, treat every bundle as a live recipe drawn from those
counts, deduct/recompute/cascade on any sale, return, restock, or order edit, per location and
across every channel, and escalate the judgement calls (negative component, partial return, backorder
vs hide, reserve for already-promised kits).

The heading screams the use case ("the bundle sale that never touches your component stock"); the H1
carries the high-intent search phrasing ("How to automatically sync bundle inventory with component
stock on Shopify"). Target phrases: "Shopify bundle inventory not updating," "Shopify bundle
inventory sync," "Shopify bundle components not deducting," "Shopify bundle overselling," "sync
bundle stock Shopify," "Shopify kit inventory tracking," "Shopify bundle component inventory," and
"Shopify bundle shows sold out in stock."
