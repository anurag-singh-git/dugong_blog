## New post

**Dispatch Nº 68 — "The deleted product that 404s your customers"**
`the-deleted-product-that-404s-your-customers.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-25 · ~2,206 words

Chosen because auto-redirecting deleted products is a high-volume, frequently-complained Shopify SEO
pain the existing 20 dispatches had not covered, and it sits squarely in the on-brand SEO lane the blog
already runs (meta descriptions, image alt text, product descriptions). It validates strongly on public
forums and help content: the recurring question is, almost verbatim, "how do I make Shopify
automatically redirect a deleted product" and "my deleted products are 404ing and hurting SEO," and the
standard answer is a version of "Shopify does not do that, you set up the redirect yourself or use an
app." The native gap is the crux and is confirmed: deleting a product creates no redirect, the URL just
returns a 404; the same silent break happens on a handle change and an unpublish; the native fix is a
manual one-URL-at-a-time redirect under Online Store > Navigation > URL Redirects (or a CSV you have to
assemble by hand); and Shopify Flow has no action that creates a redirect, so the step that fixes the
404 is the step the free tool cannot take. A whole app category exists purely to fill the gap (301
Redirect / Fix 404, SEOWILL/SEOAnt 404 Link Redirect, SC Easy Redirects, Doc404), which by itself
proves the pain is real.

The heading screams the use case for anyone living it ("the deleted product that 404s your customers"),
pulling merchants who churn catalog (discontinued lines, seasonal items, duplicate cleanups, handle
renames) and later find a wall of 404s in Search Console, and it carries the high-intent searched
phrasing around "deleted product 404," "redirect deleted product Shopify," and "Shopify 404 errors SEO."

It is deliberately adjacent to but distinct from existing posts. The angle is link equity and relevance:
the deleted page had earned a ranking, backlinks, and internal links, and a 404 drains that value
instead of passing it on, while a blanket homepage redirect is a soft 404 that loses the value anyway.
That ties it to the **meta-description** and **image-alt-text** posts (the same blank-or-wrong-field SEO
family Shopify stores but will not handle), and to the **sold-out-collection** post (what happens to a
product as its status changes decides storefront performance). The differentiator beyond "install a
redirect app": not a blanket forward, but a read of each broken URL (what the product was, by type,
collection, tags, title), a match to the closest live page, a 301, a recheck so the target never chains
into a fresh dead end, and high-value URLs held for a human.
