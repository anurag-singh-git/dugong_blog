## New post

**Dispatch Nº 70 — "The price change you make one product at a time"**
`the-price-change-you-make-one-product-at-a-time.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-28 · ~2,446 words

Chosen because bulk repricing a catalog is one of the highest-volume operational complaints among
Shopify merchants and the existing 22 dispatches had not covered it. It validates strongly on public
forums: the Shopify Community has recurring, direct threads like "Any way to increase all prices of
store by %?", "Adding a certain $$ amount to all products", "How can I bulk update product prices in
my online store?", and "Bulk price change without using an App", and the recurring story is a merchant
who needs to move every price after a supplier cost increase or a tariff and finds no native way to do
it by rule.

The native gap is confirmed and concrete: the bulk editor only lets you type values (no percentage,
no fixed-amount, no rounding math), shows products a page at a time (commonly fifty) so large catalogs
mean batch after batch, and does not expose variant prices in the same view as the parent; the CSV
export-and-import route does the math but turns repricing into a fragile spreadsheet job that can
overwrite live fields; Shopify's percentage price adjustment exists only under Markets and applies one
percentage to a whole market catalog, not a selective reprice of the home catalog by vendor, collection,
tag, or margin; and Shopify Flow has no action that sets a product or variant price at all. A whole app
category exists purely to add math to the price field (Smart Bulk Price Editor, Bulk Price Editor Pro,
Sami Bulk Price Editor, and others), which by itself proves the pain is real.

The heading screams the use case for anyone living it ("the price change you make one product at a
time"), and carries the high-intent searched phrasing around "Shopify bulk price change," "bulk edit
prices Shopify," "increase all prices by percentage Shopify," "Shopify bulk editor 50 products," and
"repricing Shopify."

It is deliberately adjacent to but distinct from the existing "the-sale-you-start-by-hand" post: that
post covers temporary, scheduled discounts that revert; this post covers permanent list-price changes
(cost increases, tariffs, margin protection, percentage and rounding rules across the catalog). The
post states the relationship explicitly and links the two in both directions. The differentiator beyond
"install a bulk price app": not a fixed rule run blindly, but resolving which products a loose scope
really means, computing per variant from cost or current price, applying the store's rounding, keeping
compare-at honest, and holding outliers (margin floors, absurd values) for a human.
