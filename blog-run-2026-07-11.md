## New post

**Dispatch Nº 81 — "The buyer who clears your whole drop"**
`how-to-limit-purchase-quantity-per-customer-on-shopify.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-07-11 · ~2,697 words

Chosen because "how do I limit quantity per customer," "stop one person buying all my stock," "why
does my two-per-order limit let someone place ten orders," and "enforce one per customer during a
launch" are among the most-repeated, still-unsolved requests for any store running drops, flash
sales, collabs, or allocation-limited products, and no existing dispatch covered it. Forum and
app-ecosystem validation is strong: the Shopify community threads ask for it verbatim, and a dense
cluster of order-limit apps (KOR Order Limits, MinMaxify, Limitsify, DMJ Cart & Checkout Limit, and
others) exists precisely because the native platform cannot enforce a per-customer cap. Shopify's own
help page confirms the native add-to-cart limit is per-cart and applies per variant independently.

The native gap is real and specific, and the post credits what Shopify does. The native add-to-cart
limit (Settings → Checkout → add-to-cart limit) genuinely caps how many of a product go into one cart
and stops an obvious single-transaction over-buy. But (1) it is per variant, not per product or per
customer, so a shopper takes the max of every color/size at once; (2) it resets on every order, living
on the cart, so the same buyer opens a fresh cart and buys again with no running total across orders;
and (3) it never looks at email, address, card, or account, and does not apply in POS, on draft orders,
or for B2B profiles. The common cart-page/theme workaround leaks because wallet buttons (Shop
Pay/Apple Pay/Google Pay), buy-it-now, saved cart links, and checkout permalinks never touch the cart
page, and underneath sits the identity problem: an account-keyed limit is beaten by a second email, a
cookie-keyed one by an incognito window. A real fix needs server-side checkout validation (Shopify
Plus Functions, or an app that uses it). The post frames the differentiator: not one more quantity
rules-table, but a read of the order + an identity match across orders (email, address, payment
fingerprint, account, device) + a decision against a running per-customer/per-window cap + enforcement
on every checkout path + cleanup (cancel/trim, refund, restock) for what slipped, with judgement
(genuine household at one address, wholesale exemption, gift order, uncertain match) escalated to a
human.

The heading screams the use case ("the buyer who clears your whole drop"); the H1 carries the
high-intent search phrasing ("How to limit purchase quantity per customer on Shopify"). Target
phrases: "Shopify limit quantity per customer," "Shopify purchase limit per customer," "Shopify max
quantity per order," "Shopify limit one per customer," "Shopify order limit app," "Shopify limit
quantity per variant," "Shopify prevent bulk buying," "Shopify per customer purchase limit," and
"Shopify add to cart limit."
