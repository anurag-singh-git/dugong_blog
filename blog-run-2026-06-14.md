## New post

**Dispatch Nº 58 — "The abandoned cart that gets one email"**
`the-abandoned-cart-that-gets-one-email.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-14 · ~1,880 words declared

Chosen because abandoned cart / abandoned checkout recovery is the highest-volume,
most-complained-about Shopify revenue leak in the merchant subreddits and the Shopify
community, and it was not yet covered by the existing 11 dispatches. It is distinct from
the adjacent recovery/comms posts: the restock post (Nº 56) is outbound back-in-stock
notification, WISMO is post-purchase tracking support, and this post is pre-purchase
recovery of carts that were filled and left.

The Shopify-specific limitations are confirmed from current research and form the crux:
(1) the native abandoned checkout automation sends a single email on one of a few preset
timers with no follow-up sequence and no branching, so a real cadence requires Klaviyo /
Omnisend or a Shopify Flow workaround; (2) the one native email is generic and cannot tell
a first-time shipping-balker from an interrupted repeat buyer, so it cannot match the
message to the reason; (3) since Shopify moved most stores to one-page checkout, the email
and phone are often only saved on full-page completion (the old three-page checkout saved
them after the first step), so many abandoned checkouts arrive with no contact at all, and
merchants below Shopify Plus currently cannot toggle back to three-page checkout. This last
point is a fresh, current complaint with hundreds of forum replies.

Statistics are sourced from current web research, not invented, and presented as
approximations/ranges in the body: average cart abandonment ~70% (Baymard, ~70.22% across
~50 studies); a single generic reminder recovers ~3% to 8%; a timed personalized sequence
across email and SMS recovers meaningfully more.

The angle beyond "install an app": recovery is a judgement-and-timing problem, not a
scheduling one. The real job is to catch every recoverable cart, recover the contact where
possible, guess the objection, match message and channel to the reason, follow up a
sensible number of times, size the incentive only when needed, stop the instant they buy,
and alert the merchant if recovery suddenly drops (a sign checkout is eating contacts). A
note callout warns against leading with a discount (it trains abandonment and erodes
margin).
