## New post

**Dispatch Nº 63 — "The product descriptions you'll never finish writing"**
`the-product-descriptions-youll-never-finish-writing.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-19 · ~1,910 words

Chosen because writing product copy at catalog scale is a high-volume, frequently-complained
Shopify pain (Reddit and the Shopify community forums are full of merchants describing hundreds of
SKUs that ship with blank or supplier-pasted descriptions, and complaints that Shopify Magic's
output "sounds like AI" and needs heavy editing). It was not yet covered by the existing 15
dispatches. It is adjacent to but clearly distinct from the meta-description post (Nº 61): that post
is the SERP snippet under ~155 characters; this post is the on-page body copy, the catalog backlog,
brand voice, and the duplicate-content problem. The heading screams the use case for anyone living
the pain ("you'll never finish writing") so it pulls searchers who feel the backlog directly.

The Shopify-specific limitations are confirmed from current research and form the crux:
(1) Shopify Magic is built into the admin and drafts from a few keywords with a tone setting, but it
operates one product at a time inside the editor; there is no native bulk button, so the known
workaround is the brittle export-to-CSV, add-a-prompt-column, re-import hack. (2) Magic writes from
the keywords you type, not from the product itself, so it does not read the variants and options, the
supplier spec sheet, the materials list, or the product image; the context that makes copy specific
still gets typed in by hand per SKU. (3) The output is widely complained about as bland and
recognizably machine-made, with no brand voice, so merchants rewrite every one and the work simply
moves from writing to editing without the catalog shrinking. (4) Nothing prevents near-duplicate copy
across similar variants, which is the duplicate-content problem from the inside, and a line pasted
from the supplier is already live word-for-word on every other store running the same feed.

The angle beyond "install a bulk AI description app": generating text was never the bottleneck.
Reading the product and sounding like you is the job. App-store one-click generators deliver speed,
but fed thin inputs they produce a large pile of generic near-twins you still have to audit and
re-edit. The Dugong playbook reads each product whole (title, type and tags, every variant and option,
existing copy, supplier spec, images), writes in one defined brand voice applied the same way across
the catalog, differentiates near-identical SKUs so each page earns its own ranking, structures each
description for SEO and scanning without keyword stuffing, writes back across the whole catalog through
the Admin API, backfills blank/placeholder pages and refreshes on change, flags the products too thin
to describe well, and logs each description with the inputs it was written from. A callout makes the
point that any tool can produce sentences; the hard part is reading the variants, spec, and photo and
sounding like a brand worth buying from.
