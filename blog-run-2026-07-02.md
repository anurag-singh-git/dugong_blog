**Dispatch Nº 74 — "The second order that arrives before the first ships"**
`how-to-combine-multiple-orders-from-the-same-customer-on-shopify.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-07-02 · ~2,054 words

Chosen because "combine / merge multiple orders from the same customer" is one of the
longest-running, most-repeated requests in Shopify merchant communities, and none of the existing
73 dispatches covered it. It validates strongly on public forums: the Shopify Community has recurring
threads titled "How can I merge orders for a specific customer to consolidate shipping," "Easier way
to combine multiple orders into a single shipping label," "How to combine multiple orders into one or
more shipments," and "How can we efficiently merge multiple orders into one." The scenario is always
the same: a customer places a second order before the first one ships, and there is no native way to
put them in one box, so the customer pays delivery twice and the merchant ships two parcels or runs a
manual workaround.

The native gap is real and specific. Shopify has no merge feature and Shopify Flow cannot merge
orders, by design: each order is a fixed financial record with its own payment capture, tax line,
discounts, fulfillment history, and audit trail, and merging would risk corrupting reporting and
reconciliation, so Shopify keeps orders immutable. The workaround it points to is a manual draft-order
routine (rebuild both orders' line items into one draft, finalize and label it, add tracking back onto
the originals, refund the duplicate shipping, cancel the originals and restock), which is six-plus
careful steps across several screens and only works if you catch the pair before either order is
fulfilled. The post frames the differentiator: not "add a merge button" but "notice the pair the
moment the second order lands, judge whether it can safely combine, ship one box, refund the fair
combined shipping, keep inventory and books straight, tell the customer once, and hold the edge cases
for a human."

The heading screams the use case for anyone living it ("the second order that arrives before the first
ships") and the H1 carries the high-intent search phrasing ("How to combine multiple orders from the
same customer on Shopify"). Target phrases: "Shopify combine orders," "merge orders Shopify," "Shopify
merge multiple orders," "combine shipping Shopify," "Shopify duplicate orders same customer,"
"consolidate orders Shopify," "refund duplicate shipping Shopify," and "second order before first
ships."

It is deliberately adjacent to but distinct from the order-and-fulfillment posts it links as cousins:
the order-change post (a reasonable change that locks at fulfillment), the tracking-number post, and
the payout-reconciliation post (where a shipping refund on one order against a combined shipment on
another must be tied back by hand).
