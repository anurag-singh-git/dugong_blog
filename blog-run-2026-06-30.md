## New post

**Dispatch Nº 72 — "The pre-order that holds your whole order hostage"**
`the-preorder-that-holds-your-order-hostage.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-30 · ~2,586 words

Chosen because pre-order and backorder handling is one of the most-complained-about Shopify
fulfillment pains and none of the existing 25 dispatches touched it. It validates strongly on public
forums and community threads: Shopify Community has live threads on splitting in-stock and pre-order
items before payment capture and on handling pre-orders without overselling, and an entire app
category (PreOrder Now, Timesact, Notify!, STOQ and others) exists purely to add a real pre-order
flow on top of the native checkbox. The recurring story is a merchant whose in-stock item gets "held
hostage" behind a pre-order item in the same cart, plus the related trap of leaving "Continue selling
when out of stock" on after stock lands, which keeps overselling and lets orders arrive untagged with
the customer unaware anything was a pre-order.

The native gap is confirmed and concrete. Shopify's only native lever is the per-variant "Continue
selling when out of stock" checkbox: it shows the same Add to Cart button (no Pre-order button, no
expected ship date), takes no deposit to charge later, and does not tag the order or line item, so a
pre-order lands looking like an ordinary in-stock sale. A mixed cart of in-stock plus pre-order items
checks out as one order with no automatic split by availability; the correct handling (partial
fulfillment, ship the ready line now and hold the rest) is a manual step taken order by order, and
nothing flags which orders need it, tells the customer the order ships in two parts, or remembers to
fulfill the held line when stock arrives. Shopify Flow can react to order creation, tag, and notify,
but has no action that splits the cart, charges a held balance later, watches inbound inventory and
fulfills the held line, emails a real ship date, or turns the toggle back off when the run sells out.

The heading screams the use case for anyone living it ("the pre-order that holds your whole order
hostage") and carries the high-intent searched phrasing around "Shopify preorder," "Shopify mixed
cart preorder," "preorder and in-stock items together," "Shopify split order preorder," "Shopify
partial fulfillment preorder," "Shopify continue selling when out of stock," and "tag preorder orders."

It is deliberately adjacent to but distinct from the inventory posts already live: the overselling
post (selling stock you no longer have) and the restock-alert post (waitlist demand on inventory you
have not connected), which this post links as its closest cousins, plus the order-change and
unpaid-order posts for the fulfillment-timing angle. The differentiator beyond "install a pre-order
app": not just adding a Pre-order button, but managing the weeks between sale and shipment, splitting
the mixed cart, holding the open line with a promised date, fulfilling oldest-first the day stock
lands, and stopping the run before it oversells, holding the edge cases for a human.
