## New post

**Dispatch Nº 65 — "The repeat customer your store treats like a stranger"**
`the-repeat-customer-your-store-treats-like-a-stranger.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-21 · ~2,000 words

Chosen because customer segmentation (auto-tagging VIPs, repeat buyers, lapsing/at-risk customers, and
discount-only buyers) is a high-volume, frequently-complained Shopify pain that the existing 18
dispatches had not covered. The merchant communities carry the recurring ask in many forms: reward
repeat buyers, flag VIPs for priority support, stop sending win-back codes to people who never lapsed,
and catch regulars before they churn. The confirmed native gap is the crux: Shopify has no native rule
that watches behavior and tags a customer when it changes. Tagging is manual by default, the one
automated path is Shopify Flow (free on every plan) which only adds a tag when a single blunt condition
becomes true (e.g. total spend over a number), and the segments feature is a filter you read rather than
a durable label the rest of the stack can act on.

It is deliberately adjacent to but distinct from the existing **order-tag** post (Nº 62). That post is
per-order tagging from the contents of one order; this post is customer-lifecycle segmentation read
across a customer's whole purchase history (recency, frequency, value, products, returns, discount use).
The heading screams the use case for anyone living the pain ("treats like a stranger"), pulling
merchants who feel that their loyal customers get the same generic blast as first-timers, and it carries
the searched phrase "repeat customer."

The angle beyond "install a segmentation app": total spend is one number and a customer is a pattern.
Two people can both have spent six hundred dollars, one a two-year loyal regular and the other a
one-time deep-discount buyer, and a spend threshold tags them identically. A spend rule also never flags
the high-value customer who has gone quiet, which is the single most recoverable signal a store owns.
The differentiator is reading the whole pattern and keeping it current: the Dugong playbook pulls each
customer's full history, reads the pattern instead of a single threshold, assigns one clear lifecycle
tag from a clean vocabulary, removes the tags that no longer fit, re-evaluates as new orders/returns/
cancellations land so a VIP who goes quiet becomes at-risk instead of a stale VIP forever, writes back
through the Admin API touching nothing else, hands the segment to the systems that act on it (email,
discounts, support priority, ad audiences), and logs each change while flagging edge cases.
