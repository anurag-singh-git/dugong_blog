## New post

**Dispatch Nº 55 — "The chargeback you lose by default"**
`the-chargeback-you-lose-by-default.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-11 · ~1,760 words declared

Chosen because chargebacks and disputes are one of the most-complained-about Shopify topics on the
merchant subreddits and the community forums, and the angle was not yet covered by the existing
seven dispatches. It is distinct from the earlier fraud post: the high-risk-flag dispatch is about a
decision at checkout (fulfil vs cancel a flagged order), while this one is post-transaction
representment (a dispute already filed, the evidence and deadline race to answer it). The recurring
forum complaint is specific and real: merchants say they had the tracking and the signature, uploaded
everything, and still lost. The piece explains why that happens.

The Shopify-specific limitation set is confirmed from current research: with Shopify Payments,
Shopify auto-populates the basic transaction data and submits a response on the deadline date, but it
does not read the reason code, match evidence to it, or write a rebuttal; the evidence window is short
(usually 7 to 21 days) and one-shot; once submitted or past the deadline nothing more can be added and
the bank's decision is final with no appeal.

Statistics are sourced from current web research, not invented: US merchants win ~54% of disputes
fought through representment and only ~17% of fraud-coded chargebacks; friendly fraud is ~75% of
chargebacks and rising at a double-digit annual rate; average disputed transaction ~$84 but all-in
cost per dispute ~$315 (fee + lost goods + shipping + labour); ecommerce chargebacks projected at
~$33.8B in 2025. Reason-code-specific evidence requirements (AVS/CVV + device/IP for fraud, tracking +
signature for item-not-received, listing + accepted policy for not-as-described) confirmed against
Shopify Help and chargeback-industry sources.
