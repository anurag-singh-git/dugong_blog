## New post

**Dispatch Nº 69 — "The tracking number you paste in by hand"**
`the-tracking-number-you-paste-by-hand.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-27 · ~2,316 words

Chosen because manually marking orders fulfilled and pasting in tracking numbers is one of the
highest-volume operational complaints among Shopify merchants, and the existing 21 dispatches had not
covered it. It validates strongly on public forums and help content: the Shopify Community has direct
threads like "how can I automate the tracking number upload process for multiple dropship suppliers"
and "how to automatically add a tracking number after fulfilling an order," and the recurring story is
a merchant fulfilling through one or several suppliers who get tracking back by email or CSV and want
to stop pasting it in by hand. The native gap is confirmed: auto-fulfillment only fires for digital
orders or items a connected fulfillment service controls; bulk fulfill marks orders shipped but still
leaves you to attach each tracking number; the CSV tracking import still needs you to assemble and map
the file (order numbers to tracking numbers to carriers); Shopify Flow has no action that fulfills an
order with a tracking number read from a supplier message; and an order assigned to a third-party
fulfillment service location will not even show the "mark as fulfilled" button. A whole app category
exists purely to fill the gap (EZ Fulfill, syncX Bulk Fulfill Tracking, DropFlow / Excel CSV Tracking
MassImport, Easy Fulfillment), which by itself proves the pain is real.

The heading screams the use case for anyone living it ("the tracking number you paste in by hand"),
pulling self-shipped, supplier-shipped, and dropship merchants who spend evenings copying numbers from
an email into a text box, and it carries the high-intent searched phrasing around "Shopify mark as
fulfilled," "add tracking number Shopify," "auto fulfill Shopify orders," and "import tracking numbers
CSV Shopify."

It is deliberately adjacent to but distinct from existing posts. WISMO covers the customer asking where
their order is (support deflection), and the order-change post covers the window between paid and
shipped; this post covers the merchant's own act of fulfilling with the right tracking. The angle is
that entering tracking is the most error-prone touch in the post-purchase flow (wrong order, dropped
digit, default carrier so the link is dead, split order marked fully shipped), and that the real job is
reading and matching a supplier message rather than data entry. The differentiator beyond "install a
tracking importer": not a fixed CSV mapping, but reading whatever shape the tracking arrives in,
matching each number to the right order and line items across split shipments, detecting the carrier so
the link works, sending one confirmation per shipment, and holding mismatches for a human.
