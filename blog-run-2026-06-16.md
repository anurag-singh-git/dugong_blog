## New post

**Dispatch Nº 60 — "The sale you have to start by hand"**
`the-sale-you-start-by-hand.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-16 · ~1,870 words declared

Chosen because scheduled/timed sale pricing is a well-upvoted, recurring Shopify community
complaint that no native feature closes, and it was not yet covered by the existing 13
dispatches. It is distinct from the adjacent commerce posts: the sold-out post (Nº 55) is
merchandising of items already out of stock, the abandoned-cart post is recovering demand after
checkout, and the discounts referenced elsewhere are coupon/cart-level offers. This post is the
timed promotion itself: setting sale prices with a struck-through compare-at across a targeted
set, scheduling the start, and reverting on time.

The Shopify-specific limitations are confirmed from current research and form the crux:
(1) Shopify lets you change a price and set a compare-at price for the strikethrough, but it has
no native way to schedule that price change to start and end on its own; the edit applies the
instant you save. (2) Shopify Flow cannot schedule a price change; the community answer to that
exact request has been "no." (3) Shopify does not remember the pre-sale price, so the revert is a
manual pass over every product touched, and a forgotten reset bleeds margin for days. (4) There
is no native way to target a sale by rule (collection/tag/vendor, minus clearance, above a margin
floor) and apply it in one move. CSV import is bulk but applies immediately, not on a schedule;
discount codes can be scheduled but show no struck-through price while customers browse. The
crowded field of sale-scheduler apps is itself evidence of the native gap.

Statistics are sourced from current web research, not invented, and presented as
approximations in the body: flash sales lift transaction rates by ~35% on average and convert
~48% better on mobile than desktop; overusing markdowns trains customers to wait for the next
discount. The framing keeps the value in precision (the exact products for the exact window)
rather than "a lower price."

The angle beyond "install an app": the timed sale is a scheduling-and-bookkeeping problem wearing
a pricing problem's clothes. The real job is to select the products by rule, record each original
price so the revert is exact, apply the discount and compare-at strikethrough while holding a
margin floor, skip/flag anything that would fall below cost or is already discounted deeper,
confirm the sale is live, revert every price at the end time with nothing left behind, and log
the run so the next sale is a one-line repeat. A callout warns that the forgotten revert is the
quiet cost: a sale that never turns off is a margin leak with no campaign attached.
