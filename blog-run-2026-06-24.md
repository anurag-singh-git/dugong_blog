## New post

**Dispatch Nº 67 — "The unpaid order that never cancels itself"**
`the-unpaid-order-that-never-cancels-itself.html` · by Priya Singh · 2026-06-24 · ~2,240 words

Chosen because auto-cancelling unpaid orders is a high-volume, frequently-complained Shopify pain the
existing 19 dispatches had not covered. It validates strongly on public forums: the recurring community
question is, almost verbatim, "how do I automatically cancel and archive unpaid orders after X days" and
"how do I cancel a pending order and unblock the inventory it is holding," and the standard answer is a
version of "Shopify cannot do that natively, you will need an app or a script." The native gap is the
crux and is confirmed: Shopify has no setting that cancels an order still unpaid after a set time,
cancelling is a manual one-order-at-a-time admin action, and Shopify Flow has no action that cancels an
order or restocks its held inventory, so the one step that matters is the step the free tool cannot take.
A whole app category exists purely to fill the gap (Easy Canceller, Kindly Payment Reminder, GO Rescue
Payments, plus a Mechanic task), which by itself proves the pain is real.

The heading screams the use case for anyone living it ("the unpaid order that never cancels itself"),
pulling merchants who take manual payment methods (bank transfer, cash on delivery, unpaid invoices,
draft orders) and watch dead orders pile up, and it carries the high-intent searched phrasing around
"cancel unpaid orders" and "pending payment."

It is deliberately adjacent to but distinct from existing posts. The angle is the inventory underneath
the pending order: Shopify commits stock to an order at placement and holds it through pending payment,
so an unpaid order freezes units a paying customer wanted. That ties it to the **overselling** post
(keeping the stock count honest) and the **restock-alert** post (freeing held stock so the next waiting
customer can buy), and to the **order-change** post (the right move depends on reading the live state of
the order before acting). The differentiator beyond "install an auto-cancel app": not a flat global
timer, but a read of each order — payment method and how it settles, new vs repeat vs wholesale, a
reminder sent before the axe, a recheck at the deadline so a late payer is never cancelled, restock in
the same step as the cancel, and high-value/wholesale orders held for a human.
